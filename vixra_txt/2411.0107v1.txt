Abstract 
In this paper, I have translated Euclid’s Element Book One into the Ewe language of Ghana, Togo and the Republic Benin. It 
is my hope that this translation will inspire researchers across Africa to translate Euclid into their respective languages.

Nyagbl ɔgl ɔwo si wotu ɖe ke me (Postulates) 
 
Mina mia gbl ɔ esiawo 
 
1.  Na ta fli-dz ɔdz ɔe tso te ƒe a ɖe ayi te ƒe a ɖe 
2.  Na ta fli-dz ɔdz ɔe si se ɖoƒe li  na edziedzi ɖe fli-dz ɔdz ɔe aɖe dzi 
3.  Na ta fli-koto si ƒe domezã kple dzidzime anye ɖesia ɖe 
4.  Be dzogoeme-blasieke ɖesia ɖe s ɔ kple won ɔewo 
5.  Be ne fli-dz ɔdz ɔe aɖe mumum anyi dzem ɖe fli-dz ɔdz ɔe eve dzi na dzogoeme si le eme 
siwo dze ng ɔ won ɔewo le sue awu dzogoeme-blasieke eve la, ne wo ta fli-dz ɔdz ɔeawo tẽ 
la, wo doa go le to si me dzogoeamewo le sue awu dzogoeme-blasieke eveawo
