1st Lipovets or Pervy Lipovets (Russian: 1-й Липовец, Первый Липовец) is a rural locality (Russian: село, lit. 'village') in Vyshnereutchansky Selsoviet Rural Settlement, Medvensky District, Kursk Oblast, Russia. Population:  178 (2010 Census); 251 (2002 Census); 


== Geography ==
The village is located on the Lyubach River (a left tributary of the Reut River in the Seym basin), 47 km (29 mi) from the Russia–Ukraine border, 45 km (28 mi) south-west of Kursk, 16 km (9.9 mi) south-west of the district center – the urban-type settlement Medvenka, 7 km (4.3 mi) from the selsoviet center – Verkhny Reutets.

Climate
1st Lipovets has a warm-summer humid continental climate (Dfb in the Köppen climate classification).


== Transport ==
1st Lipovets is located 18 km (11 mi) from the federal route M-2 Crimea Highway (a part of the European route E105), 4 km (2.5 mi) from the road of intermunicipal significance 38H-185 (M2 "Crimea Highway" – Gakhovo), on the road 38H-192 (38N-185 – 1st Lipovets — border with Oboyansky District), 33.5 km (20.8 mi) from the nearest railway halt 439 km (railway line Lgov I — Kursk).
The rural locality is situated 54 km (34 mi) from Kursk Vostochny Airport, 90 km (56 mi) from Belgorod International Airport and 237 km (147 mi) from Voronezh Peter the Great Airport.
