Mary Morton (21 March 1879 – 15 June 1965) was a British sculptor.


== Early life and education ==
Morton was born in Stroud, England on 21 March 1879. Her father was George Morton, a surgeon who was born around 1839 in the East Indies. She attended the Bristol School of Art before studying at the modelling school of the Royal College of Art between 1911 and 1913.


== Professional career ==
In 1913 Morton became a Royal West of England Academy Academician. In 1928 she became an Associate of the Royal Society of British Sculptors before becoming a Fellow in 1948. Her work was part of the sculpture event in the art competition at the 1948 Summer Olympics. She also worked closely with the Associate of the Society of Women Artists and exhibited 105 works with the society between 1913 and 1960.
Her sculpture of Charles Kennedy Scott is held by the Trinity Laban Conservatoire of Music and Dance and her woodcarving, Study of a Nude Girl beneath a Tree Carved with Foliage, is held by the Royal West of England Academy. During World War II she worked as an ARP warden at Princess Beatrice Hospital.


== Death ==
Morton died on 15 June 1965 in Sopworth House, 4 Rosecroft Avenue, Hampstead, London. At the time of her death, her estate was worth £22,900.
