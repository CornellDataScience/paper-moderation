== Stellar properties ==

Î³1 Andromedae A is a massive bright giant star with a spectral classification of K2+IIb. The star has an apparent visual magnitude of approximately 2.26. The star has been classified as a candidate post-asymptotic giant branch star. However, it does not display a chemical enhancement of s-process elements typical of an AGB star.
Î³2 Andromedae BC, with an overall apparent visual magnitude of 4.84, is 9.6 arcseconds away from Î³1 Andromedae at a position angle of 63 degrees.
Î³1 and Î³2 have an orbital period of approximately 5,000 years.
In October 1842, Wilhelm Struve found that Î³2 Andromedae was itself a double star whose components were separated by less than an arcsecond. The components are an object of apparent visual magnitude 5.5, Î³ Andromedae B, and an A-type main-sequence star with apparent visual magnitude 6.3, Î³ Andromedae C. They have an orbital period of about 64 years and a high eccentricity of 0.927. Spectrograms taken from 1957 to 1959 revealed that Î³ Andromedae B was itself a spectroscopic binary, composed of two B-type main-sequence stars orbiting each other with a period of 2.67 days. The two orbits may be coplanar. As of 2019, the angular distance between the B and C stars was 0.16 arcsecond.
